0)  PREMISE — POTENTIAL AS THE BASIS
    ------------------------------------------------------------
    Before anything definable exists, there is only POTENTIAL —
    pure logical openness for difference.

    Potential is not an entity, field, or process.
    It has no parts, intention, or duration.
    It neither acts nor contains structure.

    It is the condition where even “permission” is meaningless,
    for nothing yet exists to be permitted or denied.

    (Parallels: Aristotle’s *dynamis* understood as pure capacity;
    Heisenberg’s “potentia” and Bohm’s “implicate order”
    referenced metaphorically, not ontologically.)

------------------------------------------------------------

1)  INFINITE POTENTIAL ⇒ INEVITABLE SOMETHING
    ------------------------------------------------------------
    In unbounded potential, the set of all possible differences
    is infinite, while the configuration of no difference
    is singular.

    The null configuration has zero logical measure
    within the unbounded total of potential distinctions.
    Thus, the existence of difference follows
    as logical necessity — not as event or probability.

    (Formal analogies: measure-zero argument in set theory;
    Ramsey theory inevitability; combinatorial completeness
    within ZFC-style infinite cardinalities.)

------------------------------------------------------------

2)  DIFFERENCE AS LOGICAL PRIMITIVE
    ------------------------------------------------------------
    Difference is the minimal relational primitive:
    the logical condition that something is not identical
    to something else.

    Difference is not derived, caused, or performed.
    It is definitional — the ur-relation enabling
    all further relations to exist.

    Potential and difference are not separate states,
    but complementary aspects of the same logical totality.

    (Formal analogy: primitive relation ⊢ A ≠ ¬A —
    distinction without temporal ordering.)

------------------------------------------------------------

3)  DIFFERENCE ⇒ DISTINCTIONS
    ------------------------------------------------------------
    Each difference defines at least two distinguishable aspects.
    In infinite potential, all distinctions coexist
    without sequence, hierarchy, or creation.

    Distinctions are co-inductive:
    endlessly defined through mutual contrast
    without base case or external foundation.

    (References: Aczel’s non-well-founded sets,
    co-algebraic mutual definition, and Leibniz’s
    identity of indiscernibles reformulated relationally.)

------------------------------------------------------------

4)  NETWORK OF DISTINCTIONS ⇒ RELATIONAL CONTEXT
    ------------------------------------------------------------
    All distinctions coexist within one infinite network
    of mutual reference. Meaning arises only through relation.

    The network has no central point or boundary.
    It is non-hierarchical but contains relationally dense subsets
    — local sub-contexts — where mutual definition is maximal.

    These clusters are not enclosed regions but relational densities
    within the whole, without external borders.

    (Analogies: category-theoretic networks of morphisms,
    graph-theoretic density metrics,
    Rovelli’s relational ontology,
    Wolfram’s Ruliad as total relational closure.)

------------------------------------------------------------

5)  RELATIONAL CONTEXT ⇒ CONSISTENCY PRESSURE
    ------------------------------------------------------------
    In the total web, some combinations of relations
    contradict themselves, defining the same distinction
    as both identical and non-identical.

    Such contradictions represent relational instability.
    They do not “disappear” in time;
    they become logically disconnected —
    unable to maintain mutual reference within the network.

    Coherence therefore denotes connected subsets
    of mutually consistent relations.

    (References: principle of non-contradiction;
    paraconsistent logic allowing local inconsistency;
    Shannon information theory — redundancy as zero information.)

------------------------------------------------------------

6)  CONSISTENT DIFFERENCE ⇒ LOGICAL SUBSTRATE
    ------------------------------------------------------------
    The self-consistent portion of potential constitutes
    a stable subset of relations — the LOGICAL SUBSTRATE.

    It is not dynamic equilibrium but timeless coherence:
    the consistent lattice of all mutually defining differences.

    (Mathematical analogies: complete lattices and fixed-point
    theorems; equilibrium sets without temporal evolution.)

------------------------------------------------------------

7)  SELF-REFERENCE ⇒ FEEDBACK
    ------------------------------------------------------------
    Within the logical substrate, relations implicitly contain
    reference to their own configuration.

    Each relation reflects upon itself via self-morphism:
    the structure referencing its own consistency.

    This implicit recursion is feedback —
    not a temporal loop, but structural reflexivity.

    (Connections: Gödelian self-reference,
    reflexive categories, and self-similarity
    in recursive logical forms.)

------------------------------------------------------------

8)  FEEDBACK ⇒ PERSISTENCE
    ------------------------------------------------------------
    When recursive relations remain internally coherent
    across all self-morphic reference,
    they form persistent configurations.

    Persistence is invariant self-resolution —
    a pattern that retains definitional integrity
    under all internal reflections.

    Persistence does not endure *in* time;
    it simply *is* — coherence that cannot collapse
    without contradiction.

    (Parallels: fixed-point invariance,
    cybernetic homeostasis generalized to logical form,
    and stable self-reference in formal systems.)
