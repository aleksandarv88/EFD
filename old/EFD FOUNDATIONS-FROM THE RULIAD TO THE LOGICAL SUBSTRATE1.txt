0)  PREMISE — POTENTIAL AND DIFFERENCE
------------------------------------------------------------
Before any definable thing exists, there is only POTENTIAL —
the logical openness in which distinctions are possible but
not yet drawn.

Potential is not a field, entity, or process.
It neither acts nor contains structure;
it is the allowance for DIFFERENCE.

Difference is the first fact within that allowance:
the condition that something can be not identical to
something else.

------------------------------------------------------------

1)  NECESSITY OF DIFFERENCE
------------------------------------------------------------
Within unbounded potential, "no difference" is a singular
configuration with zero logical measure among all possible
configurations.

Therefore, difference is logically inevitable —
not an event, but a consequence of unbounded possibility
itself.

------------------------------------------------------------

2)  DIFFERENCE AS PRIMITIVE
------------------------------------------------------------
Difference (Δ) is irreducible.
It is not caused or derived; it is the relation that makes
relations possible.

Potential and Difference are complementary:
Potential allows Difference; Difference defines Potential.

------------------------------------------------------------

3)  DISTINCTION — DIFFERENCE REFERENCING DIFFERENCE
------------------------------------------------------------
Each Difference implies at least two distinguishable aspects.
When differences reference one another, a field of distinctions
arises — a co-inductive web where each part is defined only
through contrast with others.

No base element exists; all definitions are mutual.
This field is logically complete yet free of hierarchy.

------------------------------------------------------------

4)  RELATIONAL CONTEXT
------------------------------------------------------------
All distinctions coexist within one relational totality.
Meaning arises only through relation; isolation has no
definition.

This total web has no center or boundary —
only local densities where relations interconnect more tightly.
These dense subsets form contexts of coherence inside the
whole.

------------------------------------------------------------

5)  CONSISTENCY PRESSURE
------------------------------------------------------------
Within the total network, some relational combinations
contradict themselves — defining the same distinction as both
identical and non-identical.

Such contradictions cannot sustain mutual reference;
they drop out of the coherent web.

Consistency acts as logical pressure, favoring stable
connectivity.

------------------------------------------------------------

6)  LOGICAL SUBSTRATE
------------------------------------------------------------
The subset of mutually consistent relations constitutes the
LOGICAL SUBSTRATE — the stable lattice of differences that can
co-exist without contradiction.

It is not equilibrium in time but timeless coherence:
a pattern of relations that logically holds together.

------------------------------------------------------------

7)  SELF-REFERENCE AND FEEDBACK
------------------------------------------------------------
Within this substrate, relations may reference their own
configurations.

This reflexivity is FEEDBACK — not a temporal loop, but
structural self-relation.

Feedback expresses the substrate’s capacity to evaluate its
own consistency.

------------------------------------------------------------

8)  PERSISTENCE
------------------------------------------------------------
When recursive relations remain coherent under all internal
reference, a PERSISTENT CONFIGURATION arises — a pattern that
retains identity across self-reflection.

Persistence is not endurance in time; it is invariance under
self-consistency.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
NOISE — SYMMETRY-DRIVEN NON-INVARIANCE (ATEMPORAL)

In highly symmetric regions, multiple relational resolutions are equally valid;
nothing internal uniquely selects one over the others.

Define NOISE as NON-INVARIANCE UNDER EXTENSION:
a local, purely relational property may hold within one coherent subcontext
yet fail in another overlapping, equally coherent subcontext of the same substrate.

This is not randomness and not temporal change. It follows from:
• symmetry-induced underdetermination (many valid resolutions), and
• overlap of subcontexts (choices constrain one another without a unique global tie-break).

From a finitely relational, internal standpoint, this presents as “flicker”:
inclusion/exclusion of particular relations across compatible coherent views
of the same timeless whole.

Noise emerges from Difference, Context, and Consistency alone — no extra primitives.
